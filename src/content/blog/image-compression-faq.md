## 1. Lossy vs. Lossless: What's the Difference?

These are the two fundamental approaches to making images smaller:

*   **Lossless Compression:** Cleans up metadata and inefficient coding without touching pixels. The image looks *identical* ÔÇö bit for bit. Typical savings: **10ÔÇô20%**.
*   **Lossy Compression:** Intelligently merges similar colors that your eye can't distinguish. Savings of **70ÔÇô90%** are common. This is what most people need for websites, email, and social media.

> **Rule of thumb:** Use lossy for web photos and social media. Use lossless for archival, medical imaging, or screenshots with fine text.

## 2. Will Compression Make My Photo Blurry?

Not if you use a modern tool. Today's algorithms are **"visually lossless"** ÔÇö they change the data, but your eye can't tell the difference unless you zoom in past 500%.

The old "quality slider" approach (setting JPEG quality to 20%) *will* cause visible blur. Smart compression tools like ours analyze each image individually and pick the optimal balance automatically.

## 4. Why Is My File Still Big After Resizing?

1.  **Resizing** changes the pixel dimensions (e.g., 4000px ÔåÆ 2000px). This makes the image 4x smaller in area.
