If you take photos with an iPhone, you've probably noticed they are saved as **.HEIC** files. While high quality, these files often can't be opened on Windows, Android, or older software.

## What is HEIC?

**HEIC (High Efficiency Image Container)** is Apple's version of the HEIF format. It uses modern compression technology to save photos at half the file size of a JPG without losing quality. This saves massive amounts of space on your phone.

## The Compatibility Problem

Despite its benefits, HEIC is not universally supported.
*   **Windows:** Requires paid extensions to view natively (in older versions).
*   **Websites:** Most forms (passports, job applications) only accept JPG or PNG.
*   **Android:** Older devices cannot open them.
