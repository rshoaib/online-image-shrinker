## The Privacy Problem with Most Free OCR Scanners
Most "free" OCR apps and websites operate on a hidden cost model. When you upload a picture of a receipt, a contract, or a handwritten note, that file is sent to a remote cloud server for processing. 

This presents several major red flags:
1. **Data Retention**: Many services state in their Terms of Service that they can keep your uploaded documents to "improve their services" or train AI models.
2. **Security Risks**: If you are scanning sensitive documents like invoices or tax forms, uploading them to unknown third-party servers is a massive security risk.
3. **Usage Limits**: Cloud processing costs money, so "free" tools quickly hit you with strict daily limits or force you to upgrade to a premium plan.

## Tips for the Best OCR Results
While modern OCR is incredibly powerful, it still relies on the quality of the source image. 

* **Ensure Good Lighting**: Shadows and harsh glares can confuse the scanner.
* **Keep it Flat**: Warped pages from thick books are harder to read. Try to flatten the page as much as possible before taking the photo.
