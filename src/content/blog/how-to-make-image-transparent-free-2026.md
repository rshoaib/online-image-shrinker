## The Issue with "Free" Cutout Tools
Most tools that claim to make your image transparent have significant strings attached. Before you upload a photo to the first tool you find on Google, be aware of the common traps:

1.  **Low-Resolution Paywalls**: They will flawlessly make your background transparent, but when you click 'Download', they only provide a blurry, 500-pixel version. If you want the HD version, you have to pay.
2.  **Privacy Risks**: Cloud-based tools process your images on external servers. If you are uploading portraits of family members or unreleased product designs, you are giving a third party access to your private files.
3.  **Watermarks**: Many tools sneak a visible watermark (e.g., "Edited by FreeCutout.ai") onto the corner of your newly transparent image.
