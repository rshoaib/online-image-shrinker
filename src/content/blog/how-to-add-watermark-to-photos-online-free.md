## Why You Should Watermark Your Photos
When you publish images online, they become susceptible to right-click saving and unauthorized reuse. A watermark serves two main purposes:
1.  **Deterrence**: It explicitly shows that an image is claimed and protected.
2.  **Brand Awareness**: If an image is shared or reposted, your logo or website name travels with it, driving traffic back to your business.

## The Problem with Traditional Watermarking Apps
Most free watermarking apps come with catches that ruin your workflow:
*   **Forced Registration**: They interrupt you mid-task to ask for an email address.
*   **Resolution Paywalls**: They let you add the watermark, but force you to pay to download the high-resolution version.
*   **Privacy Risks**: They upload your photos to cloud servers, which is a major concern if you are working with unreleased product shots or personal photos.
